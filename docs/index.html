About KLSBench

Large Language Models (LLMs) have shown limited success on low-resource historical languages. Korean Literary Sinitic (KLS) is a low-resource language with connections to modern Chinese (shared characters) and Korean (60% vocabulary overlap).

No comprehensive benchmark exists for evaluating LLM performance on KLS. We present KLSBench, comprising 7,871 instances across five tasks: classification, retrieval, punctuation, natural language inference, and translation.

The dataset uses data from Joseon Dynasty civil service examinations and the Four Books (å››æ›¸), providing a unique challenge for understanding historical texts requiring cultural knowledge and logical reasoning.

Key Insights

  • Models fail on tasks requiring cultural knowledge despite potential cross-lingual advantages
  • Understanding Literary Sinitic requires specialized training on historical context
  • Classical rhetoric goes beyond simple cross-lingual transfer from modern Chinese or Korean
